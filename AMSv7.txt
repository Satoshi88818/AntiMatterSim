""" nuclear_evaporation.py (v7) ============================ Extended from v6 with: 1. **Proton, neutron, triton** evaporation channels (in addition to α and d). 2. **Gilbert-Cameron level densities** — more accurate nuclear temperature model using energy-dependent a(E*) with pairing correction. 3. Barkas effective-charge Bethe formula for all heavy fragments (p, d, t, α). Gilbert-Cameron Level Density ------------------------------ The nuclear temperature is now derived from the Gilbert-Cameron composite formula: For E* < E_x (matching energy): ρ(E*) = (1/T₀) exp((E* - E₀)/T₀) (constant-temperature region) For E* ≥ E_x: ρ(E*) = (π/12) a⁻¹/⁴ (E* - δ)⁻⁵/⁴ exp(2√(a(E* - δ))) (Fermi gas) The Fermi-gas level density parameter: a(E*) = ã [1 + (κ/E*)(1 - exp(-γ E*))] where ã = A/8 MeV⁻¹, κ ≈ 5.0, γ ≈ 0.05 MeV⁻¹. Pairing energy δ: δ = 12/√A for even-even nuclei (both A and Z even) δ = 0 for odd-A nuclei δ = -12/√A for odd-odd nuclei References ---------- A. Gilbert & A.G.W. Cameron, Can. J.
